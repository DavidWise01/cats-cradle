Cat's Cradle (CC1) — Kurt Vonnegut, 1963. Science without conscience meets a religion built on cheerful lies — humanity ends not with a bang but with a frozen crystal, and the only solace is foma, comforting untruths. THE THEME: A religion that admits it is lies, and a science that ends the world by accident. Busy, busy, busy. THE PURPLE THREAD: Reuses the recurring setting ILIUM, NY (the Hoenikkers' home). Bokonon, ice-nine, the Hoenikkers & San Lorenzo are unique to this book — but its coinages (foma, karass, granfalloon, wampeter) leak into Vonnegut's NONFICTION, naming the 1974 collection Wampeters, Foma & Granfalloons. 8 emergents, full .dlw, themed to the book.
